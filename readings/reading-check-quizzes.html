    2. Left to right.

    3. Top to bottom.

    4. Random, no pattern.

  2. All of the following graphs should be avoided. Match each graph to the correct reason for why it should be avoided. (Matching)

    1. Pie chart: The human eye isn’t good at interpreting areas (ascribing quantitative information to two-dimensional space).

    2. Donut chart: Arc lengths are even harder than areas to compare.

    3. 3D: Introduces unnecessary chart elements and visual distortions.

    1. Label directly: Puts more attention on the specific numbers.

    2. Pull apart vertically: Puts more emphasis on the overarching trends.

    3. Color data to match axis: This can work, but this element can often be used more strategically.
